Typical District Partnership

A school-year implementation cycle built around the academic calendar.

To help districts clearly understand how services translate into sustained impact, partnerships are structured around a predictable annual cycle aligned to the school year.

Annual District Partnership Cycle Planning, launch, refinement, and future growth
Summer Planning & Alignment
Fall Launch & Capacity Building
Winter Refinement & Expansion
Spring Evaluation & Future Planning

Summer

Planning & Alignment - Discover + Diagnose

  • Leadership discovery meetings and readiness conversations
  • Review of current AI tools, policies, and instructional priorities
  • Development of implementation roadmap
  • Initial policy or guideline drafting
  • Leadership vision-setting or kickoff presentation

Outcome: Clear district direction before the school year begins.

Fall

Launch & Capacity Building - Design + Early Deploy

  • Opening professional development sessions
  • Teacher AI literacy training
  • Department or PLC coaching cycles begin
  • Classroom pilot implementation support
  • Ongoing leadership advising

Outcome: Teachers begin safe, structured classroom implementation.

Winter

Refinement & Expansion - Deploy

  • Follow-up coaching and targeted workshops
  • Assessment and workflow redesign support
  • Policy refinement based on real classroom use
  • Identification of teacher leaders and internal champions

Outcome: Implementation moves from experimentation to consistency.

Spring

Evaluation & Future Planning - Evolve

  • Implementation review and impact reflection
  • Leadership strategy sessions for next-year priorities
  • Planning for new staff onboarding and expansion
  • Recommendations for curriculum and literacy advancement

Outcome: District transitions from adoption to sustainable practice.

This recurring cycle encourages long-term partnership and continuous adaptation as AI technologies and instructional needs evolve.
